Bun Bo Hue

A bold central Vietnamese speciality inspired by Hue, the former imperial city known for depth, fragrance and culinary precision.

StyleHue speciality
FlavourAromatic and bold
AddressOdborů 3, Prague

Bun Bo Hue comes from the historic city of Hue in central Vietnam, a place long associated with imperial culture, refined cooking and expressive flavours.

The dish is known for its aromatic broth, rice noodles, beef and fresh herbs. Compared with gentler noodle soups, Bun Bo Hue is usually more pronounced, with a deeper savoury profile and a confident fragrance.

Hue cuisine is famous for detail: the way ingredients are layered, the way aroma is balanced, and the way strong flavours are made elegant rather than overwhelming.

This page introduces Bun Bo Hue as part of the Vietnamese culinary story behind our menu. It is a dish for guests who want to explore beyond the most familiar pho bowls and discover one of central Vietnam's great specialities.

Inside the bowl

  • Aromatic broth
  • Beef
  • Rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs
  • Hue tradition
